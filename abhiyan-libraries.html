Abhiyan Libraries

Starting in 2019, Nalanda operates 20 rural community libraries in Wardha, serving as dynamic learning hubs for young learners.

prof. Abhiyan Humane founder of abhiyan labs & libraries (1976-2019)

Honoring a Legacy

The libraries are named after our friend and colleague Late Prof. Abhiyan Humane, the founder of Nalanda Labs in Wardha, as a tribute and a reminder of his contributions to education and community development.

Access to Knowledge

In many rural areas, access to educational resources like books and study materials can be limited. By establishing Abhiyan Libraries in Buddha Viharas, Nalanda Academy aims to bridge this gap and provide local students with easy access to a wide range of learning materials.

Community Empowerment

By collaborating with local communities to establish these libraries, Nalanda Academy empowers the community members to take ownership of their learning environment.

Fulfilling Dr. Ambedkar’s Vision

Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar envisioned Buddha Viharas as centers of knowledge and enlightenment. By setting up libraries in these Viharas, Nalanda Academy honors his vision and contributes to the realization of his ideals.

A team of Nalanda educators, students and volunteers, visit these libraries every Sunday to teach young students basic English, Math, Geography, Computer, Arts, Design and Science.

  • 1. Dhammek Buddha Vihar, Sewagram, Wardha
  • 2. Shravasti Buddha Vihar, Rotha, Wardha
  • 3. Triratna Buddha Vihar, Dahegaon (Miskin), Wardha
  • 4. Bahujan Buddha Vihar, Anji Mothi, Wardha
  • 5. Dhyanjyoti Buddha Vihar, Dhanora, Wardha
  • 6. Jetvan Buddha Vihar, Kamthi, Wardha
  • 7. Nalanda Buddha Vihar, Khairi, Wardha
  • 8. Vishakha Buddha Vihar, Paloti, Wardha.
  • 9. Ramai Buddha Vihar, Station Fail, Wardha
  • 10. Jetwan Buddha Vihar, Yelakeli, Wardha
